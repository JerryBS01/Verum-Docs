Verum: Your Truth to Unbiased News

A Chrome extension designed to help you uncover bias and verify the accuracy of news articles and blogs.

What is Verum?

Verum is a powerful Chrome extension that analyzes news articles and blogs for bias and accuracy.

  • Bias detection
  • Accuracy scoring
  • Easy-to-read insights

How Does Verum Work?

Verum uses advanced algorithms to scan articles and evaluate their credibility.

  1. Scans the article for key phrases and claims.
  2. Compares the content against verified databases.
  3. Generates a bias and accuracy score.

How to Install Verum

Installing Verum is quick and easy. Follow these steps to get started:

  1. Go to the Chrome Web Store.
  2. Search for "Verum."
  3. Click "Add to Chrome."
  4. Pin the extension for easy access.

How to Use Verum

Using Verum is simple. Here's how you can get the most out of it:

  1. Open any news article or blog.
  2. Click the Verum icon in your browser toolbar.
  3. View the bias and accuracy analysis.
  4. Explore detailed insights and sources.

How to Use üìù

  1. Open a News/Blog Post
    Open any news article or blog post you want to analyze for bias.
  2. Activate the Extension
    Activate the Verum extension by clicking on the extension icon in your browser.
  3. Click "Start Analyze"
    Once the extension is activated, click the "Start Analyze" button to begin the analysis process.
  4. Wait for Analysis to Load
    Allow a few seconds for the tool to process and analyze the content. Verum will start evaluating bias and contextualizing the content.
  5. See Highlights and Bias Indicator
    You will see different parts of the webpage being highlighted to indicate where the bias lies. A bias indicator will appear in the pop-up, showing whether the article is biased or unbiased.
  6. Color-Coded Bias Dots
    Bias reasons are displayed as color-coded dots. Each dot corresponds to a specific part of the article that's highlighted. Hover over any highlighted section, and the tool will show you an in-depth analysis of why it's biased (or unbiased).
